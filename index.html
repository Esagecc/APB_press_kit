DIRECTOR’S STATEMENT

The movies that I'm drawn to usually have two elements in them; intense dramatic confrontation and direct characters and stories. These are the movies that excited me when I watched them. What excited me more was watching them with my friends and family react to those intense and dramatic scenes that I loved. I love seeing their reaction and as a writer/director, I want to create those same reactions in my movies.

I love the film noir genre because it's direct on who's good and who isn't and as the viewer you always root for the good. Noir movies bring that out of us, from love affairs, money heists, fraud to psychological

I wanted to have a film that put forth a narrative that represents strong characters that are represented group in law enforcement: women and LGBTQ. Holly took on the lead role and portray herself a determine and strong homicide detective. I believe representation matters, and LGBTQ and women should have roles that empower them.

PRODUCTION TEAM BIOGRAPHIES

ROBERT CANTU
Producer, Writer & Director

Robert Cantu was born in Corpus Christi, TX, and raised in Houston, TX. In 1998, he moved to Los Angeles and he started out as a still photographer on countless student and amateur films. He produced his first short film “5150”, an inspired film from Stanley Kubrick’s 2001, and it was entered in numerous film festivals. In 2001, he produced and directed “The Barista”. In 2003, Robert produced and directed an ego time bomb detective with no moral principles, “Detective Grgich”, a noir film. In 2004, his first real short film “Suspended Homicide” met his professional standard and he directed with greater confidence. He has written my first full-length screenplay “corpus FUSION”. He writes three more full-length screenplays: “Dirty Mary, Crazy Larry”, “Silent Motive” and “JASONLAND”. In 2019, Robert produced and directed a horror short film, “Jason Z”. A Youtube released was on June 13, 2019. By 2020, Robert produced and directed two short Noir films, “The Florentine Gardens” and “Homicidal Crimes” released in 2021. Both films were selected for various film festivals.
